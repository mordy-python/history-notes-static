Chapter 17: Manifest Destiny

Wed 21 April 2021 by Mordy Waldner

William Henry Harrison is elected in 1840

Harrison wasn't the best of leaders so the following 2 men really led the country - Daniel Webster was Harrison's secretary of state - Henry Clay was leader of the whig party Harrison made an hour long speech at his inaugauration in the pouring rain and a month later dies in office Congress had no idea what to do - They decided the VP becomes POTUS John Tyler was now in office

Clay vs. Tyler

  • Clay and Webster wanted to pass a bill that would make the bank under Govt. Jurisdiction but Tyler Vetoed it again
  • A watered down version of the bank bill was also vetoed by Tyler Eventually Clay and Webster kicked Tyler out of the Whig party telling him hes not a real whig

Relations with England

In 1837 the Canadians wanted indepence from England and the US openly supported the candian rebels

The Caroline was a US ship carrying weapons to the Canadians - The British stopped and searched the ship and sank

The Creole was a US ship carrying slaves - There was a slave rebellion on board and they sailed the ship to british controlled bahamas - They were welcomed with open arms adn told they could live as free ppl there

All these 3 events strained the Anglo-American relationship

Skirmishes were starting to break out on the canadian-maine border about where the border line was Webster and Ash-Burton decided to define the border between Maine and Canada

Texas

Mexico and Mexicans still viewed Texas as a part of Mexico - Mexico said to the US that if the US annexed or even recognized Texas it would be viewed as an act of war Texas was making treaties with Holland, Belgium, and France who recognized Texas as an independent country England realized that acknowledging Texas would weaken the stability of the US
